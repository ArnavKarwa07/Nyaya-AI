Research Papers related to Indian Legal AI Project

1. Domain-Partitioned Hybrid RAG for Legal Reasoning: Toward Modular and Explainable Legal AI for India (2026)
This paper proposes a modular architecture that integrates specialized RAG pipelines for the Supreme Court, statutes, and the IPC with a Neo4j Legal Knowledge Graph. It uses an agentic orchestrator to enable multi-hop relational reasoning beyond simple semantic similarity.
Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23371
2. NyayGraph: A Knowledge Graph Enhanced Approach for Legal Statute Identification in Indian Law using Large Language Models (2025)
The authors develop a domain-specific Knowledge Graph of the Indian Penal Code (IPC) encoding cross-references and structural relationships from official reports. This provides models with explicit structural knowledge often missing in text-only training.
Link: https://aclanthology.org/2025.nllp-1.11.pdf
3. ReGal: A First Look at PPO-based Legal AI for Judgment Prediction and Summarization in India (2026)
This research introduces the ReGal framework, which utilizes reinforcement learning (Proximal Policy Optimization) to iteratively refine legal decisions and explanations. It uses reward signals derived from legal textual alignment to handle high-stakes, long-document tasks.
Link:https://www.researchgate.net/publication/398979748_ReGal_A_First_Look_at_PPO-based_Legal_AI_for_Judgment_Prediction_and_Summarization_in_India
4. NyayaRAG: Realistic Legal Judgment Prediction with RAG under the Indian Common Law System (2025)
Proposes a Retrieval-Augmented Generation framework that emulates courtroom scenarios by providing models with factual case descriptions alongside retrieved statutes and precedents. It focuses on producing legally faithful explanations aligned with common law reasoning.
Link: https://aclanthology.org/2025.ijcnlp-long.92.pdf
5. Nyay-Darpan: Enhancing Decision Making Through Summarization and Case Retrieval for Consumer Law in India (2025)
This study presents a specialized framework for the consumer law sector, employing sector-based classification and hierarchical summarization. It integrates lexical and semantic similarity matching to retrieve relevant precedents efficiently for dispute resolution.
Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.06090
6. TathyaNyaya and FactLegalLLaMA: Advancing Factual Judgment Prediction and Explanation in the Indian Legal Context (2025)
Introduces a large-scale, fact-centric dataset for Indian legal judgment prediction. It evaluates models on their ability to predict outcomes and generate interpretable explanations based strictly on factual narratives.
Link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.04737
7. LegalSeg: Unlocking the Structure of Indian Legal Judgments Through Rhetorical Role Classification (2025)
This paper provides a massive dataset of 1.4 million sentences labeled with seven rhetorical roles like Facts, Arguments, and Rulings. It is critical for the hierarchical parsing and semantic segmentation features of your project.
Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.05836
8. Integrated Legal AI Reference Architecture (ILARA): Synthesizing Multimodal Architectures for the Post-IPC Indian Legal Landscape (2026)
Evaluates multimodal architectures (RAG, OCR, and Automated Drafting) designed to assist citizens and lawyers during the transition from the IPC to the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS). It provides a practical implementation roadmap for India's legal ecosystem.
Link:(https://rjwave.org/ijedr/viewpaperforall.php?paper=IJEDR2601600)
9. Incongruence Identification in Eyewitness Testimony (2025)
Develops the MIND dataset and the INTEND framework to identify contradictions and discrepancies within complex witness narratives. It uses 6Ws and multi-hop reasoning to pinpoint the exact spans where testimony incongruence occurs.
Link: https://www.arxiv.org/pdf/2502.05650
10. Document Matching for Contradiction Detection in Low-Resource Legislative Texts (2025)
Employs transformer models (XLM-RoBERTa) and KeyBERT clustering to identify conflicting meanings between legislative sentences. This is highly relevant to your project’s core feature of cross-act conflict detection.
Link:(https://www.researchgate.net/publication/394708938_Document_Matching_for_Contradiction_Detection_in_Low-Resource_Legislative_Texts_With_Self-Training_and_Augmentation_Using_Transformer_Model)
11. MILDSum: A Novel Benchmark Dataset for Multilingual Summarization of Indian Legal Case Judgments (2023)
Presents a pioneering effort in cross-lingual summarization of English legal documents into Hindi. It includes over 3,000 judgments summarized by legal practitioners, addressing the language barriers inherent in the Indian judiciary.
Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.18600
12. ILDC for CJPE: Indian Legal Documents Corpus for Court Judgment Prediction and Explanation (2021)
Introduces the foundation dataset for Indian court judgment prediction, containing 35,000 annotated Supreme Court cases. It establishes the benchmark for explainable AI in the Indian common law context.
Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.13562
13. NIT-Surat@L-Summ: A Semantic Retrieval-Based Framework for Summarizing Indian Judicial Documents (2025)
Experiments with sequence-to-sequence models and semantic retrieval to produce abstractive summaries of voluminous judgment texts. It addresses domain-specific challenges like abbreviations and unstructured legal formatting.
Link: https://aclanthology.org/2025.justnlp-main.14.pdf
14. Automated Legal Document Drafting in the Indian Judicial System (2025)
Investigates the use of NLP and transfer learning for transcription, entity extraction, and court-standard template generation. It focuses on reducing the manual burden of document preparation through jurisdiction-aware automation.
Link: https://www.ijraset.com/research-paper/automated-legal-document-drafting-in-the-indian-judicial-system
15. Advantages of Domain Knowledge Injection for Legal Document Summarization: A Case Study in English and Hindi (2026)
Focuses on injecting domain knowledge into generative models through continual pre-training on large legal corpora. It demonstrates statistically significant improvements in factual consistency for English-to-Hindi legal summarization.
Link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.07382
